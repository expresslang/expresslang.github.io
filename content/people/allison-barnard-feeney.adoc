== Key Contributor to EXPRESS Standardization

Allison Barnard Feeney is a founding member of the EXPRESS Language Foundation and a
key contributor to the standardization of EXPRESS within ISO 10303 at NIST
(National Institute of Standards and Technology).

== Contributions

Feeney played a central role in the development and standardization of the EXPRESS
language and STEP (Standard for the Exchange of Product model data) at NIST,
helping bridge the gap between research and industrial adoption of the standards.

Her work at NIST was instrumental in the transition of EXPRESS from a research
project (PDDI) to an international standard (ISO 10303).
