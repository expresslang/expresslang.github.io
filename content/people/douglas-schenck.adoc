== Inventor of EXPRESS

Douglas Schenck is the original inventor of the EXPRESS information modelling language,
created during the PDDI (Product Data Definition Interface) program in 1982 at
McDonnell Aircraft (now merged into Boeing), initiated by the US Air Force.

== Contributions to EXPRESS

Schenck conceived and designed the EXPRESS language to facilitate "`expressiveness`"
in modelling information — a need that arose from the challenges of exchanging product
data across the aerospace supply chain.

His key contributions include:

* *Invention of EXPRESS* (1982) — the foundational information modelling language
* *Co-author* of _Information Modelling: The EXPRESS Way_ (Oxford University Press, 1994), the authoritative reference manual on EXPRESS, co-authored with Peter Wilson
* *Project leader* of ISO 10303-11:1994 (first edition), together with Prof. Bernd Wenzel, guiding the language through its international standardization at ISO

== Impact

EXPRESS has become the backbone of industrial data exchange, used globally in
aerospace, automotive, construction, and smart manufacturing. Schenck's invention
has enabled decades of interoperability across the manufacturing supply chain,
and continues to power Industry 4.0 and digital twin applications.
