== Vice-President, EXPRESS Language Foundation

Hiroshi Murayama is a founding member and current Vice-President of the EXPRESS
Language Foundation. He is CEO of SATS Inc. (Standard Applied Technologies and
Services), based in Ibusuki, Kagoshima, Japan.

== Career

Hiroshi began his career as a nuclear engineer at NAIG (Nippon Atomic Industry
Group), which was absorbed into Toshiba Corporation in 1990. He worked on
nuclear reactor design, including geometric designs for anti-seismicity and
automated analytical model extraction.

From late 1989 to early 1991, he studied computational geometry at École des
Mines de Saint-Étienne in France, under Prof. B. Peroche with academic advisory
from Dr. Dominique Michelucci (now Professor at the University of Bourgogne and
INRIA).

After returning to Japan, he served as Chief Research Scientist and then Research
Fellow at Toshiba's R&D center until his retirement from the company in 2019.
He subsequently founded SATS Inc. in 2020 to support international
standardization efforts.

== Contributions to Standards

Hiroshi has been active in ISO and IEC standardization since the mid-1990s:

* *ISO TC 184/SC 4* — expert since 1996, initially in WG2 for geometric shape
  and product property information exchange, working closely with Prof. Guy
  Pierra
* *Convenor of ISO TC 184/SC 4/JWG 24* (since 2018)
* *Deputy Convenor of ISO TC 184/SC 4/WG 25* (since 2022)
* *Chair of IEC SC 3D* (2008–2017), during which he authored IEC 61360-2,
  IEC 62656-1, and IEC 62656-5

His technical specialties span nuclear engineering, shape design, anti-seismic
design of civil infrastructure, product data dictionaries and ontology, and data
modelling in general.
