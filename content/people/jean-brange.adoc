== Président, AFNeT Services

Jean Brangé is a founding member of the EXPRESS Language Foundation and
Président of AFNeT Services, a French organization focused on digital
interoperability standards for industry. With a background in architecture, he
brings a unique perspective bridging building design and industrial data
exchange.

== Career

Jean studied architecture and mathematics at the École nationale supérieure
d'architecture de Paris-Belleville and Université Paris 7. He began his career
at RFR (Rice Francis Ritchie), the celebrated structural engineering firm, where
he worked on computer simulations from 1992 to 1995.

Since 2000, he has led major digital integration programs in industry, serving
as Director of AFNeT from 2000 to 2011. He also taught at the École Spéciale
d'Architecture from 1999 to 2007.

He is currently Associé at AFNeT Services, focusing on SaaS solutions for
standards-based interoperability, and was previously Associé at Boost SAS
(2004–2021).

== Contributions to Standards

Jean is a leading figure in the STEP community in Europe, particularly around
STEP AP242 and long-term digital archiving (LOTAR). He leads the EasySTEP and
STEP Next initiatives, which aim to:

* Democratize the exchange of complex industrial data through the STEP standard
  (ISO 10303)
* Provide a universal, free, and open-source STEP viewer (Apache 2.0 license)
  supporting AP242, AP209, AP210, PLCS, and more
* Build a community-driven vision with no vendor lock-in

He is actively involved in the TDP (Technical Data Package) message
standardization project for the European Aerospace and Defence domain, and
leads platform projects for standards interoperability and PLM.

His specialties include PLM, secured collaboration, management of large
projects, simulations, STEP, and ISO 8000.
